## Course Arc
- Sessions 1-4: Greek and Roman warfare (massed infantry, phalanx to maniple, siege engineering)
- Sessions 5-6: Medieval warfare (stone fortifications, castles, siege engines, longbow/crossbow, Agincourt)
- Sessions 7-8: Gunpowder revolution and Ottoman expansion
- Sessions 9-10: Napoleonic Wars
- Sessions 11-16: 19th c. through WWII (Crimea, Civil War, WWI, WWII land/sea/air/amphibious)
- Sessions 17-18: Nuclear weapons and precision warfare
- Sessions 19-20: Future of warfare (space, AI, network warfare)

## Thomas's Analytical Framework (from syllabus)
- Five recurring themes: mass, mobility, adaptability, supply chains (logistics), operational leadership
- Tension between "bias toward offense" vs. "advantage of the defense" — appears throughout
- "Western way of war" as organizing concept (but Asian innovations examined for their influence)
- "Demodernization" thesis applied to current conflicts (Ukraine, Sudan, Middle East)
- Variables for analyzing battle outcomes: terrain, tactics, weather, technology, culture of combatants
