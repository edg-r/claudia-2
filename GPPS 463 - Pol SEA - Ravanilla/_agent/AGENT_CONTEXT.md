### W3 Discussion Question (Approved)
"Stubbs argues that the specific security threats Southeast Asian states faced during the Cold War, from communist insurgencies to external pressures, played a decisive role in shaping their development strategies. Given that Dell and Olken and Acemoglu show how colonial extraction left lasting institutional legacies, how much of the variation in postcolonial development across the region can we actually attribute to differences in Cold War security pressures rather than inherited colonial institutions?"

**What worked:** Correctly identified the tension between the three readings. The Stubbs security pressures angle was the right framing to set up the question.

## Edgar's Writing Rules for Discussion Questions

These are hard rules. Follow them every time.

1. Keep it SHORT. 2-3 sentences max. Do not over-explain.
2. Never use emdashes. Use commas, periods, or semicolons instead.
3. Never use the "it's not X, it's Y" or "is it A or is it B" rhetorical framing. State the argument directly.
4. Do not add a "why it matters" sentence that reintroduces a binary framing. Let the question land on its own.
5. Conversational seminar tone, not academic paper tone.
6. General tendency: I overcook. Pull back. Be more direct, less elaborate.
