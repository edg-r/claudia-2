## 2026-04-24 — "Paper" means continuous argument, not taxonomy
When Edgar asks for a research paper, he means a continuous-argument document with a direct thesis stated in the opening, evidence marshaled across paragraphs, counter-evidence acknowledged, and an implementation or conclusion section that earns its place. He does not mean a bullet-pointed taxonomy with section headers and exemplars slotted underneath. v1 of the advanced-AI-workflows paper was rejected for exactly this: "I want to see how others are using AI in my landscape... a fully fleshed out paper with a direct thesis on the way things are going, and some potentially actionable points." Paragraphs are the body. Bullets only at the very end in an action list, and only if they earn it.

## 2026-04-24 — Don't benchmark Edgar's own setup
When researching a landscape Edgar operates in, Edgar is the audience, not the subject. Do not tell him his system is original, idiomatic, or publishable. Do not treat Claudia as the case study the paper is validating. Mention Claudia-like patterns as part of the landscape where relevant, without flattery. He knows what he has.
