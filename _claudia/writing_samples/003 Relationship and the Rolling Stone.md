People in my life have always come and go. Often I am the one who leaves. I ask if its better to be left or to do the leaving. I guess i'm more used to the latter. But the one who leaves always has to face the change of going. Bob Dylan talked about the rolling stone. Its an apt analogy, but it never really talks about how the rolling stone was shaped. I think to the guy on instagram that kicks the rock until it is a sphere. Initially jagged and sharp, the rock slowly forms itself into a sphere. Initially reluctant to move, it scrapes the ground, its friction preventing it from going very far. After many kicks, eventually it rolls easily. Maybe too easily. 

I wonder if I roll on too easily, if I am at the stage of a perfect sphere. Arguably better than before, shaped by my experiences. So that I may roll where the world will take me. Perhaps I have lost that which allows me to stop. To create friction with the ground. I take my pictures and move on. 

I will always make friends where I am, I just cant help it. But will I ever make a strong enough connection, to stop rolling. The problem is a sphere is uniform, it has no uniqueness. If I am kicked too far, parts of me will be scattered like dust all over the world. Unable to coalless again. Small parts of me left behind. I grow smaller and smaller. Until there is little left to kick.
