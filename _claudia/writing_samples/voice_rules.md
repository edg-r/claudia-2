# Edgar — Voice Rules

Load before drafting or editing in Edgar's voice. Full profile: `voice_profile.md`.

## Do

- **Open with circumstance, then claim.** Lead with "With...," "After...," "By...," or a declarative. Recommendation in the first two sentences of any memo section.
- **Land paragraphs on short sentences.** Step rhythm down; end on a rim shot.
- **Concrete verbs of motion and force.** Coalesce, bypass, countermand, kick, roll, percolate.
- **Keep the agent visible.** "The President should," "the ROK must." Never "it is recommended."
- **Triadic lists** with parallel form.
- **Carry one metaphor through a piece.** If a figure appears twice, preserve both; it is load-bearing.
- **Return to the opening frame at the close.** Do not rewrite closing sentences without reason.
- **Preserve capitalized theory terms** (Principal, Agent, Monitoring and Reporting) as technical signals.
- **Cite in-text, APA 7**, inside the paragraph.

## Don't

- **No emdashes. Ever.**
- **No "it's not X, it's Y."** State the positive claim.
- **No throat-clearing** ("It is important to note," "This memo will argue").
- **No semicolons or colons mid-sentence** unless the sentence demands it. Colons fine as section headers.
- **No passive voice dodges.**
- **Do not over-formalize his memo voice.** "Should request" stays "should request."
- **Do not flatten personal prose into academic register.** Fragments and "I wonder / I guess" transitions are deliberate.
- **Do not cut terminal fragments** in personal prose.
- **Do not rewrite working sentences for token savings.** Voice-replacing rewrites get rejected.

## Silent Fixes

Spell "therefore" and "lens" correctly. Restore apostrophes in memo register. In personal drafts, fix apostrophes via margin comment. Fix agreement and typos silently.

## Register Switch

Personal → Memo: "I" becomes the institutional agent; opening sentences lengthen; capitalized theory terms and APA 7 citations appear. Holds: circumstance-first openers, triadic lists, physical verbs, short terminal landings, return-to-frame endings.
