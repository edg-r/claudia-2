# APA 7th Edition Citation Standard

When any agent output references academic sources -- readings, journal articles, books, chapters, reports, or web sources -- the output must include properly formatted APA 7th edition citations.

## In-Text Citations

Use parenthetical author-date format throughout the body of the output:

- One work: (Author, Year)
- With page number: (Author, Year, p. 14)
- Two authors: (Author & Author, Year)
- Three or more authors: (First Author et al., Year)

## Reference List

Place a **References** section at the end of the output, immediately before the output disclosure block. Format rules:

- Heading: `## References`
- Alphabetical order by first author's last name
- Use hanging indent style: first line flush left, continuation lines indented (in Markdown, indent continuation lines with two spaces)
- Italicize titles of journals, books, and volumes
- Use DOIs as links when available

## Common Reference Types with Examples

### Journal Article

Last, F. M., & Last, F. M. (Year). Title of article. *Journal Name*, *Volume*(Issue), Page--Page.
  https://doi.org/xxxxx

Example:

Acemoglu, D., & Robinson, J. A. (2001). The colonial origins of comparative development: An empirical
  investigation. *American Economic Review*, *91*(5), 1369--1401. https://doi.org/10.1257/aer.91.5.1369

### Edited Book Chapter

Last, F. M. (Year). Title of chapter. In F. M. Last (Ed.), *Title of book* (pp. Page--Page). Publisher.

Example:

Fearon, J. D. (1995). Rationalist explanations for war. In R. J. Art & R. Jervis (Eds.), *International
  politics: Enduring concepts and contemporary issues* (pp. 60--82). Pearson.

### Book

Last, F. M. (Year). *Title of book*. Publisher.

Example:

Krugman, P. R., Obstfeld, M., & Melitz, M. J. (2018). *International economics: Theory and policy*
  (11th ed.). Pearson.

### Web Source

Last, F. M. (Year, Month Day). Title of page. Site Name. https://url

Example:

World Bank. (2023, June 15). World development indicators. The World Bank.
  https://databank.worldbank.org/source/world-development-indicators

### Government or Institutional Report

Organization Name. (Year). *Title of report* (Report No. if applicable). Publisher or URL.

Example:

Congressional Research Service. (2024). *U.S.-China strategic competition in Southeast Asia*
  (CRS Report No. R46781). https://crsreports.congress.gov

## When to Include References

Include a reference list whenever the output:
- Summarizes, paraphrases, or quotes from a specific reading or source
- Draws on claims attributed to named authors
- References assigned course readings by name

Do not include a reference list for purely procedural outputs (task logs, status updates, scheduling confirmations) that do not engage with academic content.
