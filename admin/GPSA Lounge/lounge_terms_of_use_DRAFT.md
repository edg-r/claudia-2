# GPSA Graduate Student Lounge — Terms of Use

Welcome to the GPS Associates Graduate Student Lounge. This is a shared space, stood up by graduate students for graduate students. It runs on a small budget and a lot of goodwill. These terms keep it usable for everyone.

## 1. Who the Lounge Is For

The lounge is for currently enrolled GPS graduate students. Access is tied to your active GPS enrollment. If you leave the program, your access ends.

## 2. What the Space Is For

Studying, quiet work, meals, coffee breaks, small group meetings, and GPSA-sanctioned events. It is not a classroom, a reservable private office, or overnight shelter. Treat it like a shared living room for the program.

## 3. Hours & Access

- **Weekend hours:** [TBD — committee]
- **Summer hours:** [TBD — committee]
- The lounge is not 24-hour. Someone closes it each day.
- Do not prop doors or share access credentials. If you are the last one in the room at closing, you are responsible for closing out per Section 5.

Nearest restrooms: Shores Diner and Taco Villa.

## 4. Kitchen, Fridge & Food

- **Label your food** with your name and the date. Unlabeled items are subject to removal.
- **Fridge purge:** [TBD — committee] day each week. Anything left after purge goes in the trash.
- The main fridge is provided by The Pantry and is also the central pickup point for CSA boxes. Leave room for CSA deliveries and do not move CSA boxes that are not yours.
- A possible second fridge is [TBD — committee].
- Wash your dishes when you are done. Do not leave them in the sink.
- Clean up spills immediately. Wipe down counters and the table after you eat.
- **Kitchen cleaning cadence:** [TBD — committee].
- No open flames, hot plates, or personal appliances beyond what is already provided.
- **Alcohol:** [TBD — committee].

## 5. Upkeep & Closing

- Department representatives rotate responsibility for daily and weekly upkeep. The current rotation is posted inside the lounge.
- **At closing**, the closer runs the sign-off checklist: dishes washed or in the drying rack, trash and recycling taken out if full, tables wiped, chairs pushed in, microwave wiped, lights off, door locked. Sign the clipboard.
- **Restore the space** to how you found it. Furniture moved for a meeting goes back. Whiteboards get erased.

## 6. Capacity

Comfortable capacity is approximately 20 people. Posted hard cap: [TBD — committee]. Do not exceed the posted number. If an event is pushing the room past capacity, move it.

## 7. Guests, Meetings & Events

- Small drop-in guests (a visiting friend, a classmate from another program) are welcome when the room is not full. You are responsible for your guest.
- Meetings and events that will occupy a majority of the space need advance coordination so they do not shut out drop-in users. Reservation process: [TBD — committee].
- Events that serve food or drink must clean up immediately after. Trash out, surfaces wiped, furniture reset.
- No lounge furniture, kitchen equipment, or supplies leave the room.

## 8. Quiet & Shared Use

- Default volume is library-plus: conversation is fine, phone calls and video calls should use headphones or move to a hallway.
- During peak study periods, the lounge runs quieter by convention. Read the room.
- Do not monopolize a table with belongings when you are not there. Unattended items left overnight may be moved to lost and found. Unclaimed items may be removed after [TBD — committee] days.

## 9. Changing These Rules

These terms are a living document. Proposed changes go to the GPSA board through your department representative. Material changes (hours, access model, budget use, enforcement) require a GPSA vote. Minor operational tweaks (cleaning rota, signage) can be made by the committee and posted.

## 10. Disputes

If you have a conflict with another user, try to resolve it directly first. If that does not work, bring it to your department representative or the GPSA board. The board mediates. The board's decision is final for lounge-level disputes. Conduct issues that rise to university policy are referred out.

## 11. Governance

The lounge is operated by GPSA under UCAB. Day-to-day stewardship sits with the GPSA Lounge Committee during stand-up. Governance transitions to the standing GPSA structure in **Fall 2026**, at which point department representatives become the primary operational link between users and the board.

## 12. When Rules Are Broken

Enforcement runs on a ladder, not a switch:

1. **Verbal reminder** from any user or rep.
2. **Written warning** from the GPSA board.
3. **Temporary suspension** of lounge access (length scaled to the issue).
4. **Referral** to the appropriate university office for conduct that falls outside lounge-level response.

Damage, theft, or repeated violations move faster up the ladder. Replacement costs for damaged equipment or supplies may be assessed.
